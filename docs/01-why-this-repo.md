# Why this repo exists

## The short version

My father spent his entire career working with NGOs. I grew up in the orbit of that work — the underfunding, the paperwork, the hours that disappeared into coordination that had nothing to do with the mission. I never worked in the sector myself. I am not a case worker, a programme officer, or a field operator. I would not pretend to do that work.

What I am is a builder. I have spent twenty years in enterprise data — master data management, data governance, integration, quality. I spend my weekends building things: apps for my daughters, learning platforms for data professionals, training tools. The building habit is the thing I have to contribute.

This repo is what happens when those two facts meet.

## The slightly longer version

Agent-based tools like [Hermes Agent](https://github.com/NousResearch/hermes-agent) are genuinely new. They are not just chatbots. They sit on a server, they remember things, they run tasks while you sleep, they respond on the channels your team already uses — WhatsApp, Slack, Telegram. The leverage for a small team is real.

They are also complicated. Installing one is not hard. Using one well — knowing what to ask it to do, what *not* to trust it with, how to hand it off to a colleague who wasn't in the room when it was set up — that is hard. Most small nonprofits do not have someone on staff who can do that.

I can do that. My day job is enterprise-scale data management. Doing it for a small NGO running an iftar drive is a much smaller problem than the ones I solve during the week. So: why not?

## What this repo is not

- **Not a consulting pitch.** If a skill in this repo saves your organisation an afternoon, that's the outcome I want. No sales funnel, no call-to-book.
- **Not a framework to describe.** There are plenty of decks already that explain why AI could help nonprofits. This repo tries to do something much smaller and more concrete: give you specific, working skills for specific, common problems.
- **Not my NGO-sector credentials.** I do not have any. If the content in a skill feels wrong for your context, tell me, and I will fix it — I would much rather hear "this doesn't work for a refugee intake desk because X" than guess and ship something misleading.
- **Not a solo project.** Every skill here is in the agentskills.io open format. It runs on Hermes, it runs on OpenClaw, and it will run on the next open agent runtime that ships. If you work on agentic tools and want to contribute skills for the nonprofit context — please do.

## The philosophy

> *Anyone can describe a system. I'd rather hand you a working one and say — here, try it.*

That's what the whole repo is trying to do. Each skill is a working thing. Try it. If it helps, good. If it doesn't, tell me why, or fork it and make it yours.
